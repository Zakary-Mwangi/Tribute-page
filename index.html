Professor Wangari Maathai

Founder of the Green Belt Movement.Winner of the nobel peace prize in the year 2004.

Professor Wangari Maathai an environmentalist, the founder of an organisation known as The Green Belt Movement whose main aim was to plant trees and environmental protection. She was the first African woman from Kenya to be awarded the nobel peace prize in the year 2004.


Here's a timeline for professor Wangari Maathai life.

  • 1940: Born on1 April 1940 in Ihithe village,Nyeri county in Kenya.

  • 1956: She completed her education at St. Cecilia's primary school as the best student which gained her admission in Loreto High School in Limuru, the only Catholic high school for girls in Kenya by that time.

  • 1960: She was a beneficiary of the Kennedy Airlift program that supported promising students to go and further their studies in the United states. The program was funded by John F.Kennedy, then a United States senator and a selfless Kenyan politician Tom Mboya played a crucial role proposing ways to make education in Western nations available to hardworking students in Kenya and Africa.

  • 1964: Received her Bachelor of science degree from Mount St.Scholastica college(now Benedictine College).

  • 1966: Received her masters degree in biological sciences at the university of Pittsburgh and was appointed to a position as research assistant to a professor of zoology at University of Nairobi.

  • 1969: She was married to Mwangi Mathai.

  • 1971:She became the first Eastern African woman to receive a Phd in veterinary anatomy from the University of Nairobi.

  • 1975: She became the senior lecturer in anatomy while teaching at university of Nairobi.

  • 1976: In, she was sent to the first UN conference known as Habitat I by UNEP.

  • 1977 : On 5 June 1977, during theWorld Environment Day, Wangari Maathai led the National Council of Women of Kenya (NCWK) on the outskirts of the city in planting seven trees in honor of the historical community leaders. This gave birth to The Green Belt Movement, an organization she founded whose main aim was tree planting, environmental conservation and advocating for women rights.

  • 1979: She separated with her husband Mwangi(a member of parliament) after a lengthy separation. It was said that Mwangi believed that Wangari was too “strong-minded for a woman”and that he was “unable to control her. He named her as “cruel” and also accused her of adultery with another member of parliament in court filings. Shortly after the trial, Wangari suffered from high blood pressure and she believed that the judge ruled in the favor of Mwangi. Wangari referred to the judge as either incompetent or cruel in an interview which led to her six months imprisonment in lang’ata women prisons but she was luckily released as her lawyer formulated a statement that the court found sufficient for her release.

  • 1989: She learned of a plan by the government to construct a magnificent 60-storey Kenya Times Media Trust Complex in Uhuru Park and also to include a large statue of president Daniel Arap Moi. She protested against the plan as it would undermine the environment. However the government and the parliament could not listen to her demands and said that the project was opposed by ignorant few and that she was crazy. The project commenced and during the first speech by the president regarding the project the president stated that those who opposed the project had “insects in their heads”. He also suggested that wangari Maathai should keep quiet and respect men as per African traditions. Despite the government efforts, her protest and media coverage helped her to triumph as the foreign investors canceled the project in january1990.

  • 2002: She was elected as a member of parliament winning with 98% of the votes.

  • 2004: Wangarĩ Maathai was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for her contribution to sustainable development, democracy and peace.

  • 2010: She wrote a book Replenishing the Earth(Spiritual Values for Healing Ourselves and the World). The book stressed the importance of working hard, collaborating together in order to make a positive change.

  • 2011: Died on 25 September 2011 of complications arising from ovarian cancer while receiving treatment at Nairobi hospital.
